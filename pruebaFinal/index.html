Information about Temple of Guayaquil - Ecuador

The doctrine of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints that has to do with temples originates in 1832, two years after the organization of the church, when its founder Joseph Smith teaches what Latter-day Saints Days considered as a divine revelation, in which the desire to build temples was referred to. In 1836 Smith and church members complete the Kirtland Temple, the first church temple, in the city of Kirtland, Ohio.

first congregation of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Ecuador was organized in 1965. By 1992 this movement had 90,000 members in Ecuador, mainly in Quito, Guayaquil and Otavalo. For members of the LDS movement, the centers and the orders and ceremonies held there as the constant exposition of the belief in Smith's vision are one of the most memorable moments.

On April 3, 2016, President Thomas S. Monson President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints announces the construction of a second center in Ecuador in the city of Quito.
